How to properly wear a cloth face mask

By Alexandra White

October 5, 2020

As the COVID-19 virus has remained a threat to society, it is imperative that we attempt to protect ourselves and others from the virus. Although the virus is airborne and highly contagious, wearing a mask can significantly reduce the rate of transmission between individuals. Public health experts at the Center for Disease Control believe that mask use is a form of source control. In other words, maks can prevent the spead of water droplets that travel through the air when coughing, talking or sneezing because it acts as a physical barrier. According to the National Institutes of Health, COVID-19 deaths could be reduced by 65%, if 15% of the public uses a highly effective mask.

Cloth masks are a great option for people who are not medical workers. However, if you plan on using a cloth mask, follow these guidelines:

According to the CDC, cloth masks should cover your nose and mouth. It should secure snuggly under your chin and have no gaps. After selecting a face mask try it on and make sure it stays in place as you talk. Some have used cloth gaiters instead of a face mask, but there is no evidence that a gaiter is an effective barrier.

Cloth masks should have at lease two layers of fabric. Sometimes three layers is also encouraged, however, two layers will suffice. Make sure that the cloth used in the mask is a breathable fabric like cotton. A fabric like vinyl can make it harder to breath,so avoid this material.

Cloth masks must be cleaned regularly. It can be added to laundry and should be dried on high in the dryer. Masks can be washed by hand but there are som caveats. The CDC recommends using a bleach solution to clean masks by hand. However, John Hopkins Medicine affirms that masks can be cleaned by hand in hot, soapy water for at least 20 seconds. Nevertheless, hand washed masks must be dried on high in a dryer.
